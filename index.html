Gisèle Scanlon

Vice President of GSU

My experience as GSU Vice President working with and for you, shaped my ‘I CARE’ philosophy. As your President, I will continue to develop Innovative solutions, create online and offline Community spaces, promote Accessibility of services, support Research and advocate for Equality by working with postgraduates, College and National policy makers.

  • My student life in Trinity

    The Trinity student experience, both inside and outside lectures, has revolutionised my understanding of myself, others and the world. I started out in the GSU (Trinity Graduate Students’ Union) as an enthusiastic GSU Class Representative in the School of English in 2016/17 and I was deeply honoured to be elected as GSU Vice President last year.

  • My life in the GSU

    I have used my four years in the GSU to listen and respond to the needs and concerns of students and I work tirelessly to represent you, your space and your rights at all times and in all circumstances, I want to take everything I’ve learnt and accelerate positive change across Trinity. I am running for President because I CARE.

  • Innovation
  • Graduate Students’ Union Journals
  • July 12, 2019 ongoing

I have shown a commitment to innovation. I took on the responsibility this year of managing two teams who were working on the GSU owned publications, namely College Green Journal, a literary journal and Trinity Postgraduate Review, a peer reviewed academic journal. I partnered with a former GSU President Shane Collins to negotiate taking these journals online with the Dean of Research’s Office and the Library as it became apparent that it would offer more opportunity to postgraduate researchers getting published. As GSU President I will publish these two Journals in the Autumn online and show how Innovation is not simply about technology or business, it is about doing things differently and better and with creativity. I will push the College to develop a culture of innovation which responds to challenges with creativity, imagination, collaboration and ambition and contributes to progress on climate targets and the UN SDGs. I will also champion a scheme to recruit postgraduate talent to look at publishing and innovative solutions to COVID-19.

  • Community
  • Christmas Commons
  • 10th Dec 2019

I have shown a commitment to Community. I invited four staff members for honorary GSU membership at our annual GSU Christmas Commons and a Trinity Community of academics professionals, and students celebrated with The Dean of Graduate Studies Neville Cox; Dean of Students Kevin O’Kelly; Ciara Joyce – who serves as the postgraduate liaison officer of the Student Counselling Service – and assistant librarian Niamh Brennan honorary membership. At the time of Christmas Commons the draft of the new Strategic Plan committed to undertake the systemic renewal of postgraduate education and these GSU Honorary members are the embodiment of the building blocks of that idea.

  • Community
  • Christmas Market
  • 4th Dec 2019

Trinity College had never held a Christmas Market before and to raise much needed funds for the PG Student Assistance Fund (Hardship Fund) Trinity College I emailed the Provost on 10/10/2019 and asked him if we could host a Christmas Market on the night on the Christmas tree lighting to raise much needed funds. I also asked if he’d promote Irish when lighting the Christmas tree. Both happened and student stall holders kept their market proceeds and collection buckets used throughout the event raised a healthy figure for the Student Assistance Fund.

  • Accessibility
  • Postgraduate Spaces
  • Dec 5, 2019

I have shown commitment to accessibility. I care deeply about the spaces we share as a community and the fact that some students still lack full access to facilities like kitchen space or study space or common rooms. As spaces was of deep importance to me this year as I was also the GSU PG Spaces Officer I brought some very practical solutions to improve the welfare of the postgraduate community. I learnt first-hand how adding microwaves and fridges and seating and food cupboards can really improve the student experience. Some of you who are my friends with disabilities could not come to the 1937 Reading Room this year because it is wheelchair inaccessible and I promise I will try to fix that. As GSU President, I am committed to working with Disability Services to change this and to publish a new long-term accessibility strategy for the postgraduate community. I am committed to widening access and ensuring the diverse experience and needs of postgraduate students are reflected in University policy and planning.

  • Research
  • Research Integrity and Impact Module
  • Jan 26 2019

I have shown a commitment to research. Through revision the format and content of the 5 ECTS structured PhD module in Research Integrity and Impact in an Open Scholarship Era for research students will transform this year. This mandatory online module which is taken across the three faculties was developed last year and through our student feedback we expressed a desire for the module to radically transform. The library informatics officer Niamh Brennan and the Dean of Graduate Studies welcomed this authentic feedback and inputs from our postgraduate community and I was invited to help on this pitch to bring change. Thanks to your valuable feedback, we were able to secure funding to employ a dynamic team to help develop, redesign and rebuild this module from the ground up to enhance the student user experience for incoming doctoral postgraduates this coming year. A Big “Thank You!”

  • Equality
  • Free Hot Water
  • Jan 17 2020

I have shown a commitment to equality. I worked through the Christmas holidays this year to support students who don’t celebrate it. I noticed that the only hot water supply available to postgraduate students free of charge over Christmas was what Estates & Facilities and the Library had made available in the 1937 Reading Room to postgraduates. What started out as an accessibility issue (it limits movement of postgrads), became for me about equality. The negotiations I had around Free Hot Water across campus involved clever thinking from the Catering Services around how best to facilitate everyone. Many of us use the Free Hot Water because we’re on budgets.
